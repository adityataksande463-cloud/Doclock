#1

Look at a Bowl

5–15 minutes
beginner student employee overthinking

Essence

Gaze at an empty bowl – the simplest beginning. The mind, focused on a single form, becomes still. The emptiness inside the bowl mirrors the emptiness inside you.

How To Do It

  1. Place an empty bowl (or any simple object) in front of you at eye level, about an arm's length away.
  2. Sit comfortably with your spine erect. Close your eyes for a moment, then open them.
  3. Gaze at the bowl without blinking, as long as comfortable.
  4. Look as if you have never seen a bowl before – with total freshness, without naming or judging.
  5. Notice its shape, its color, the way light falls on it. Just see, don't think.
  6. If your eyes tire, gently close them and visualize the bowl in your mind's eye. Then open and gaze again.
  7. Continue for 5–15 minutes. When thoughts come, simply return to gazing.

Why It Helps

  • 👨‍🎓 Student: Builds concentration in a gentle way – perfect before studying.
  • 👔 Employee: A quick focus reset during the workday; clears mental clutter.
  • 😥 Overthinking: The steady gaze slows down the mental chatter, bringing you to the present.
  • 🆕 Beginner: The simplest possible technique – no instructions to remember, just look.
