#2

The Fullness of Breath

5–15 minutes
breath energy beginner employee

Essence

When the breath is completely in – at the top of inhalation – or completely out – at the bottom of exhalation – at those two points, the breath pauses naturally and energy becomes full. Become aware of that fullness. That moment is a door.

How To Do It

  1. Sit comfortably with spine erect. Close your eyes.
  2. Take a few natural breaths to settle.
  3. Bring your awareness to the breath. Notice the cycle: inhale – pause – exhale – pause.
  4. This time, focus on the moment when the inhalation is complete and the lungs are full. Just before you start to exhale, there is a split second of fullness. Feel that.
  5. Then shift to the moment when the exhalation is complete and the lungs are empty. Just before the next inhale, feel that emptiness as fullness of a different kind.
  6. Alternate between these two points: fullness at the top, fullness at the bottom. Don't force; just notice.
  7. Continue for 5–15 minutes, gently resting your attention on these turning points.

Why It Helps

  • 👨‍🎓 Student: Helps you become aware of subtle energy, improving focus and vitality.
  • 👔 Employee: A quick way to recharge during the day – feeling the fullness of breath brings fresh energy.
  • 🧘 Seeker: Introduces you to the tantric understanding of prana – life force.
  • 😥 Anxious: The rhythm of the breath calms the nervous system; focusing on the turnings points anchors you in the present.
