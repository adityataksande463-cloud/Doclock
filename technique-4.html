#4

The Navel Center

5–15 minutes
energy grounding seeker employee beginner

Essence

The navel is the center of the body, the source of vital energy – in tantra, it is where kundalini awakens. By resting awareness here, you tap into your inner fire, your grounding, and your life force. The navel breathes with its own rhythm.

How To Do It

  1. Sit comfortably with spine erect, or lie down. Close your eyes.
  2. Take a few deep breaths to center yourself, then breathe naturally.
  3. Bring your attention to your navel – the area a few finger-widths below the navel (the hara or dantien).
  4. Feel the gentle rise and fall of the belly with each breath. Don't control it – just observe.
  5. Gradually, become aware of warmth, pulsation, or energy in that area.
  6. Imagine each breath flowing into and out of the navel center, energizing your whole body.
  7. If your mind wanders, gently return to the sensation at the navel.
  8. Continue for 5–15 minutes.

Why It Helps

  • 👨‍🎓 Student: Grounds scattered energy, helping you sit and study with stability.
  • 👔 Employee: Recharges your inner battery during a long workday; a quick navel focus can restore energy.
  • 🧘 Seeker: Awakens the dormant energy at the base of the spine – a foundational practice for kundalini.
  • 😥 Anxious: The navel is a grounding anchor; it pulls you out of your head and into your body, reducing anxiety.
  • 💼 CEO: Strengthens your core – literally and energetically – giving you presence and authority.
