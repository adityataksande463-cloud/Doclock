#15

Witnessing the Breath

5–15 minutes
student employee anxious beginner

Essence

Instead of controlling your breath, you simply watch it – like a witness on a riverbank watching water flow. You are not the river; you are the watcher.

How To Do It

  1. Close your eyes and take two deep breaths to settle.
  2. Breathe normally and bring your attention to your nostrils.
  3. Feel the air coming in – cool. Feel the air going out – warm.
  4. Simply watch. Do not change or force your breath in any way.
  5. If your mind wanders, gently bring it back to the sensation of breathing.
  6. Continue for 5–15 minutes.

Why It Helps

  • 👨‍🎓 Student: Trains the mind to focus on one thing – useful during studies and exams.
  • 👔 Employee: Creates a gap between you and stress; you respond calmly instead of reacting.
  • 💼 CEO: When you witness your breath, you witness your thoughts – better decisions come from that space.
  • 😥 Anxious: The breath is always in the present. Watching it pulls you out of future worries and into now.
